Job monitoring and resource estimation

Job monitoring and resource estimation#

Français

This intermediate-level workshop (CIP201) is the continuation of First steps on supercomputers (CIP101); we explore in details certain key aspects of high-performance computing.

The workshop begins with a review of the job scheduler. What is it for? What are the main commands to submit, show, cancel, and analyse compute jobs? How do we write the job scripts that drive these computations? In addition to refreshing your knowledge from the previous workshop, this section will serve as a reference during the exercises.

The next section introduces the two types of compute jobs. Serial jobs are the simplest. Parallel jobs deserve a more in-depth presentation, including the differences between multi-threaded and MPI programs. Data parallelism is briefly discussed and is the topic of a subsequent workshop, Managing large batches of jobs (CIP202).

Estimating the resources required for a compute job can be challenging! This section covers compute time, memory, and number of CPU cores. How do we determine the minimal amounts required and the optimal quantities? What is the impact of the requested resources on your jobs’ priority? What should you do if the available resources seem insufficient? GPU resources will be discussed in a separate workshop (to be announced).

Monitoring ensures that a job is making proper use of the resources it was allocated. To monitor a job, one must connect to the compute nodes to examine the running programs. To debug a job that is not running correctly or to optimise a resource request, interactive sessions are particularly useful. Finally, some clusters offer a user portal that facilitates job monitoring.

Note

This workshop was designed for guided sessions with a Calcul Québec instructor on our cloud computing platform. The files necessary for the exercises are in your home directory on the platform.

You can also follow this workshop on your own and do the exercises on any Calcul Québec or Digital Research Alliance of Canada cluster. Your jobs’ wait time, however, will be longer than on the cloud platform. Download the necessary files with the following command or browse them online:

git clone https://github.com/calculquebec/cip201 cip201-main
